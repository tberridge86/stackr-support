3. Card scanning and AI-assisted recognition

Stackr provides card-scanning functionality. When you manually scan a card in the Market area, an image of the trading card may be transmitted from your device to Stackr's backend service for processing.

For manual Market scans, Stackr may send the card image to Ximilar, a third-party image-recognition provider, to help identify the trading card. If the first identification attempt does not return a usable result, Stackr may make a further recognition attempt and/or use local or alternative matching processes to resolve the card.

Card scanning is initiated by you when you choose to use the scan feature. Stackr requests camera permission only so that you can capture a card image for scanning.

Card-recognition results may be incorrect or incomplete during beta testing. You should verify the card name, set and card number before relying on a scan result for collection or marketplace decisions.

7. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect information handled through Stackr, including the use of hosted backend and database services and the separation of private service credentials from the mobile application.

No online service can guarantee absolute security. You should protect your account credentials and notify us promptly if you suspect unauthorised account use.

Support communications and technical records may be retained for a reasonable period where required to resolve issues, maintain security or document actions taken in relation to a request.
